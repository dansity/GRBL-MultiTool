CNC Helical Boring G-Code Generator

Generates G-Code for helical boring operations to create precise holes larger than your tool diameter.

Boring Parameters
Diameter of cutting tool.
Tool Diameter is the diameter of your end mill or boring tool that will cut the material.
Final hole diameter.
Bore Hole Diameter is the final diameter of the hole you want to create. Must be larger than tool diameter.
Total depth to bore.
Bore Depth is the total depth of the hole, specified as a positive value. The tool will helix down to this depth.
Z descent per revolution.
Ramping per Turn is how much the tool descends in Z for each complete circular revolution during the helix.
Cutting feed rate.
Feed Rate is the speed at which the tool moves through the material during cutting operations.
Z height for rapid moves (G0).
Safe Z Clearance is the Z-height above the material used for rapid horizontal travel (G0) between bore holes.
Enter X and Y coordinates separated by a comma (or space/tab).
Bore Hole Locations are the X and Y coordinates of the center of each bore hole, relative to the work zero (G90). Enter one coordinate pair per line.
